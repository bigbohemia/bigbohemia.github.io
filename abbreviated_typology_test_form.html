Jungian Personality Assessment

Please answer the following questions briefly.

Questions

Decision-Making

When making decisions, do you rely more on past experiences and concrete facts, or do you prefer to imagine future possibilities? Why?

Problem-Solving

Do you focus on practical details or overarching patterns when solving problems? Give a quick example.

Time Orientation

Are you more focused on enjoying the present moment, or do you usually think about long-term goals?

Conflict Resolution

In disagreements, do you prioritize fairness and logic or harmony and feelings? Why?

Social Energy

Do you feel more energized after spending time with others or after time alone?

Thinking Style

Do you prefer to think out loud and refine ideas through conversation, or do you process ideas internally before sharing them?

Adaptability

When plans change unexpectedly, do you adjust quickly or take time to think things through?

Planning

When organizing a trip, do you make a detailed itinerary, leave room for flexibility, or avoid planning entirely?

Ambiguity

Do you act based on intuition or gather as much information as possible before making a decision?

Support Style

When a friend has a problem, do you offer logical solutions or emotional support?

Unexpected Challenges

Do you improvise in the moment or pause to realign with your values or plans when something unexpected happens?

Strengths

What is one strength you feel defines how you approach life?

Growth Areas

What’s one area where you’d like to improve or grow?
